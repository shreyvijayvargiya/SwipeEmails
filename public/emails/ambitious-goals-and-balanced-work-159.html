Ambition & Balance explores better ways to work—as individuals and as teams. If you want to accomplish ambitious goals without making yourself (or your team) miserable in the process, you're in the right place.

Here are a few of our most popular articles to get started with:

Async Communication: The Real Reason Remote Workers Are More Productive

Doist's Founder and CEO explains what async communication is, what it looks like in practice, and why it's the key to a calmer, more productive workplace—remote or otherwise. A must-read for forward-thinking leaders.

How Context Switching Is Sabotaging Your Productivity (and What You Can Do About It)

In our collective state of constant distraction and information overload, managing your attention is more important than managing your time. If you only read one article from our blog, make it this one.

How to Plan Your Day

This tactical guide will help you create a daily planning ritual to ensure you start each day feeling organized and in control and end each day feeling satisfied with what you've achieved (so you can fully disconnect from work guilt-free).

And here are some of our personal favorites:

7 Cognitive Biases That Make Us Suck at Time Management

We all act in predictably irrational ways. These "mental errors" cause us to focus on the wrong tasks, leave projects until the last minute, or work well past the point of efficiency. Use this guide to spot and avoid everything from the Mere Urgency Effect to the Sunk Cost Fallacy.

Want a More Productive Morning Routine? "Pay" Yourself First

Borrowing a concept from the world of personal finance, this article explores how to make the most of your early hours to energize you for the day ahead. In a time where the concept of work-life balance is debated at length, this piece takes clear and defiant stance: your work benefits when you put life first.

How Remote Teams Win With Retention

Companies like Google and Amazon struggle to keep employees for barely a year. Yet, remote teams boast retention rates above 95%. Learn how distributed workplaces keep their employees longer, with insights from the Doist team.
